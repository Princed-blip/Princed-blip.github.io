Selected work

In [1]:

Brain Tumor Detection (MRI Classification)

Built a deep learning system to classify brain MRI scans into glioma, meningioma, pituitary tumor, or no tumor — trained on 3,264 medical images across 4 classes. Compared two transfer-learning backbones (EfficientNetV2-B0 vs. ResNet50) to evaluate the accuracy/efficiency tradeoff.

Out[1]:

ResNet50 achieved the strongest overall performance (76.9% test accuracy, 0.939 AUC-ROC) despite being ~3.6x larger than EfficientNetV2-B0 (71.6% test accuracy, 0.893 AUC-ROC). Notably, EfficientNetV2 was better at catching glioma cases — the hardest class for both models — showing model choice isn't just about the headline number. Deployed the pipeline as an interactive Streamlit app for real-time MRI classification.
